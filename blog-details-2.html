How Local Bakeries Are Saving Thousands

Written by Sarah Jenkins Oct 08, 2025 • 4 min read

Bakeries face a unique challenge: the shelf life of their core product is measured in hours, not days. By the time the sun sets, a perfectly good sourdough loaf or a basket of croissants is considered "day-old" and often ends up in the trash.

The Cost of Freshness

For a typical neighborhood bakery, waste can account for up to 15% of daily production. Over a year, this translates to thousands of dollars in lost revenue and raw material costs. However, a new wave of digital intervention is changing the narrative.

By listing these surplus items on our app at 4 PM, bakeries like "The Golden Crust" have seen an immediate uptick in evening foot traffic. Customers who come for a discounted loaf often end up buying a full-priced coffee or a specialty cake for the next day.
